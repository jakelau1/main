On black coffee and private metaphysics

How daily rituals trick the mind into thinking it has a method.

Mar 27, 2026

Most habits survive not because they are wise, but because they are portable. Coffee is one such habit: bitter enough to feel principled, ordinary enough to be mistaken for discipline.

A decent private philosophy often begins in trivial gestures. The cup, the chair, the page, the false promise that coherence is only one more sip away.

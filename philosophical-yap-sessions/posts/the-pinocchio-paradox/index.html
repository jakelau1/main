The Pinocchio Paradox

In which me and my friend Marco try to discuss whether Pinocchio is telling the truth... over 1L of sake.

Apr 5, 2026

The paradox goes as follows: Pinocchio proclaims: "My nose grows now."

Let us assume two scenarios: one where Pinocchio is telling the truth, and one where he is lying.

In the scenario where Pinocchio is telling the truth, his nose should grow. Yet, dear reader, we know that Pinocchio's nose only grows when he is lying. So he must have been telling a lie when he said the statement, "My nose grows now." But if he was telling a lie, then his statement should be false; his nose should not grow now. This seems to rule out the possibility that Pinocchio was telling the truth.

Yet imagine the scenario where Pinocchio is lying. He proclaims: "My nose grows now." Since he is lying, his nose should grow. Yet, if his nose grows, and he claims that his nose will grow, then he is by definition telling the truth. How, then, could he possibly be lying?

Over a liter of soju, me and my friend Marco came up with two solution to this problem: we must pay attention to Pinocchio's internal state, and we must consider time as a factor. Let me explain.

Here is the first elegant solution to the Pinocchio paradox. In the situation where Pinocchio proclaims the actuality that his nose grows (i.e. wherein he proclaims that his nose will grow and it does indeed grow), it is possible that Pinocchio simply does not believe his nose will grow, yet says that it will grow. In this case, he has lied even though his statement is in fact correct, and so it is consistent with the fact that his nose has grown. Similar to how I might say, "It will rain tomorrow," despite my not believing it will rain. As it turns out, it does rain tomorrow, contrary to my initial belief. Thus, I have still lied (i.e. my internal belief regarding the statement was false), yet my statement was in fact true.

Here is another elegant solution: Pinocchio understands that his nose might grow, but not right this very moment. In this case, on a strict interpretation of the phrase, "My nose grows now," he is in fact lying. Once Pinocchio tells the lie that his nose will grow immediately, his nose subsequently grows, because his statement was in fact false.
